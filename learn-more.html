OUR story

The Sustainable fish Initiative is founded by two UC Berkeley Students who believe that sea-based food has yet to be fully realized for its potential in feeding the Human Race. With smart policies from government, education on part of the consumer, and innovation from the producers and distributers, we can solve many land-based ecological problems with the introduction of a new, local, ocean based food system.

Originally a class project for ESPM 155 (Environmental Science, Policy, and Management), this project has become more of a personal journey. We journeyed across the entire Bay Area. By interviewing the people working in the Bay Area Fish Industry and being inspired by their work, we hope that our findings will spread and influence the discussion on what role sea-based food can play in solving problems with our existing Agro-ecological food system.

THe Founders

Rachel Hall

My name is Rachel Hall and I am a fourth year student studying environmental issues, city planning, and public policy. My main focus is on understanding the environmental problems facing our generation, and how to influence others’ awareness by understanding society’s relationship with, and therefore impact on, the environment. Educating others on these intertwined socio-environmental dilemmas in any form is a daily task of mine. I hope to use my interdisciplinary background to address complex topics such as climate change in a creative and comprehensive way, considering all facets including society, environment, and the economy. Capitalizing on my undergraduate education at Cal, I plan on going to law school after a year of work experience.
Michael Chang

My name is Michael and I am a 4th year Civil Engineering and Architecture student. I grew up in an Asian community whose culture was centered around eating, and the ocean. Every weekend me and my parents would visit the local Asian market to buy (recently) live fish, thus sparking a lifelong interest in food. Since coming to Berkeley,I have to develop my interest by analyzing the distribution and production systems from a Civil Engineering context. I hope to be involved in developing the system to feed more and more people. Future plans include working in the water industry for a few years and continuing with a masters degree in Architecture.
