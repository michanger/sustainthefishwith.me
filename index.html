THE CASE FOR FISH

How many species of chicken are there in the world? Cows? Pigs? It turns out that there are about 16 domesticated species of animals in our modern agricultural system. This is not the case with Fish. We have never truly domesticated fish- in fact more than half of our fish is caught wild- the last category of animals we still hunt. Needless to say this practice has been proven more and more unsustainable. Even with this loss of biodiversity, we still have dozens of commercial varieties of fish, shrimp, lobsters, and oysters to choose from. This diversity is to be cherished, and protected. We can no longer fish the seas in order to satisfy our appetites. Raising the fish has great potential to not only reduce demand for wild fish, but also to widen access of a new, healthier source of meat to the American eater. There is evidence that high-fish diets lead to longer, healthier lives. By investing in aquaculture, we can do our planet, and our health, a huge favor.

Join us on a journey to learn the landscape of the fish culture in the California Bay Area. Its history, ties with the rest of the world, and potential future, as we see where this exciting and under-appreciated category of food may lead us.
